Citizen Science · Bloom Reporting

Report a jellyfish sighting

Spotted a bloom? Your report goes directly into the JellyWatch database. Every sighting — even a small one — helps build the picture of where and when blooms are moving.

You don't need to know the species. Just describe what you saw and where.

📍 Location tip: Most phone cameras embed GPS in photos. If you upload a photo, we can often extract the coordinates automatically. Otherwise, a city or landmark name works fine.

Quick species ID guide

🪼

Nomura's Jellyfish

Huge — up to 2m across. Brownish-purple fringe. Mainly Yellow Sea and Korea Strait. The big one.

Nemopilema nomurai
⚪

Moon Jellyfish

Flat, translucent disc with four visible rings (gonads). Fist-sized. Common everywhere. Mostly harmless.

Aurelia aurita
🟣

Mauve Stinger

Small, purple-pink, bioluminescent at night. Long tentacles. Painful sting. Mediterranean and open Pacific.

Pelagia noctiluca
❓
