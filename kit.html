🌊 Citizen Science Tool

CoastCare Kit

Everything a coastal volunteer needs to report jellyfish sightings properly — species ID guide, sample vials, waterproof field notes, and a direct link to the JellyWatch database. Assembled by hand in Seoul. Shipped free to monitors across Korea.

This kit exists because good citizen science needs good tools. A photo from a phone is helpful. A photo plus a location, a density estimate, a water sample, and a size reference is data.

What's in the kit

Eight items, all Coupang-sourced or printed locally. Fits in a large ziplock bag. Takes about an hour to assemble a batch of 20.

🦪

Laminated species ID card

Full-color, wallet-sized. Covers the four main species you'll encounter on Korean coasts: Nomura's, Moon, Mauve Stinger, and Lion's Mane. English, Korean, and Chinese on one card. QR code links to the full ID guide online.

Printed + laminated by Ethan · 코팅기
🧪

2× sample collection vials (5ml)

Small plastic vials with snap caps for collecting water samples or a small tissue fragment near a bloom site. Helps confirm species and water conditions when submitted alongside a report.

샘플 바이알 플라스틱 · Coupang
📓

Waterproof notepad (small)

Works wet. For recording density, time, location, and any observations before you have phone signal to submit. Much more reliable than trying to type notes on a wet touchscreen.

방수 노트 · Coupang
✏️

Waterproof pencil

Writes on wet paper. Pens smear; pencils don't. Simple but the difference between a legible field note and an illegible one when you're working near water.

수중 연필 · Coupang
📏

10cm ruler strip

Laminated. Place next to a jellyfish in your photo to give a size reference. A photo of a "big jellyfish" tells us very little. A photo with a 10cm ruler tells us it's a 40cm bell — which tells us a lot.

자 미니 · Coupang
📋

Folded A5 insert card

The how-to guide. Five steps to report a sighting, density scale reference, what to do with the vials, and the QR code linking directly to submit.html. Trilingual: English, 한국어, 中文.

Printed at 편의점 · folded by hand
🔗

QR code sticker

Sticks to the vials or notepad cover. Links directly to jellywatch.org/submit — so even if you lose the insert card, you can still find the reporting form.

Printed on label paper · Coupang
🛍️

Large ziplock bag

The container. Waterproof, resealable, lightweight. Everything lives in here. Fits in a jacket pocket or small bag. Labeled with the JellyWatch logo and Ethan's contact info.

지퍼백 대형 · Coupang

🔨 How these get made

Every kit is assembled by hand in Seoul — sourced from Coupang, printed at the school copy room or local 편의점, laminated with a ₩30,000 laminator. A batch of 20 takes about an hour. It's not slick. That's kind of the point.
